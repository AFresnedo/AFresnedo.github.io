My Story

I am a full stack developer, passionate about back end development. My development history began as a curiosity, in community college. At that time, I decided to find out if my ideas about coding were more than just fantasies. They were. I took two introductory courses to learn, Java and object oriented programming, and instantly fell in love with software creation.

Fast forward graduating from community college and entering the University of Washington, I wanted to continue my pursuit of computer science. However, I incorrectly felt that I was penned into doing a major in applied mathematics. Despite finishing my general education credits, and taking advantage of the opportunity to complete some CS classes (including data structures, algorithms, and databases), I did not complete my math degree. For me, Mathematics did not appear to be a lifelong pursuit.

Luckily, as an aspiring web developer, I found a project to sink my teeth into. I worked on a project called Math Affirm, for 2 months. The site was built using Ruby on Rails and, as the sole developer, I was able to gain first hand experience in creating a full stack app.

Following such a positive experience, I immediately set out to begin a career as a web developer. I was not prepared. A few frustrating months later, I recieved life changing advice from a friend, whose husband had recently made a career shift into web development.

In Seattle, I underwent a boot camp called Web Development Immersive, offered by General Assembly. GA was an overwhelmingly awesome experience. For the first time in my life, I was surrounded by people interested in the same thing as me. I became proficient in JavaScript and learned to use both JavaScript and Python as back end development languages. Suprisingly for me, I also found joy in front end development (when aided by clever and powerful libraries, such as React).

Insights Gleaned

My story has taught me...

Most importantly, that good work and good people can make all the difference in the world. Good work gives you the ability to work harder and longer, without burnout. Having good people around you keeps you motivated, where otherwise you would falter.

The struggle of dedicating myself to hard work, that I was not passionate about. In contrast, working on my first full stack application showed me how easy it was to get immersed in good work.
